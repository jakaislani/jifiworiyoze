    Who is Susan Tedeschi?

    Grammy awards winner Susan Tedeschi is an American singer, songwriter, and guitarist. She is active in the musical field since 1995. She rose to fame with her albums like Just Won’t Burn, Wait for Me, Hope and Desire, and Back to the River. She also became a judge for the 7th annual Independent Music Awards to support independent artists’ careers.

    Age, Parents, Siblings, Family, Ethnicity, Nationality

    The daughter of Dick Tedeschi, Susan Tedeschi was born in Boston, Massachusetts on 9 November 1970. She is of Italian ethnicity and has an American nationality.

    She is the granddaughter of Nick Tedeschi and great-granddaughter of Angelo Tedeschi.

    Susan Tedeschi was inspired by African-American Baptist churches.

    Since childhood, she has built up a strong interest in singing. In her childhood days, she used to sing for her family members. She often listens to her father’s record collection of old vinyl recordings of musicians like Mississippi John Hurt and Lightning Hopkins.

    In which school did Susan Tedeschi attend?

    Tedeschi graduated from Norwell High School and attended the Berklee College of Music where she sang in a Gospel Choir. At the age of 20, she received her Bachelor of Music degree in musical composition and performance.

    Susan Tedeschi: Professional Life and Career

    Susan Tedeschi is active in the field of music since 1995 and she is still active in it. In 1993, she formed a band Susan Tedeschi Band together with Tom Hambridge and Adrienne Hayes and learned to play blues guitar in Boston from musician Tim Gearan in 1995.

    She became influenced to play guitar by artists like Buddy Guy, Johnny Watson, Stevie Ray Vaughan, Freddie King, and Doyle Bramhall II. Tedeschi also released her first album Better Days in the same year. Then she released her solo album Just Won’t Burn in 1998 featuring young guitarist Sean Costello. In March 1998, she became the first artist to play Michele Clark’s very first Sunset Sessions at the Marriott in the US Virgin Islands. After onwards, she released several albums such as Wait for Me, Hope and Desire, Back to the River, and much more.

    Susan Tedeschi has frequently toured together with Trucks under the name Soul Stew Revival. Later, Tedeschi and Trucks formed a new group called Tedeschi Trucks Band in 2010. The group has released several albums like Revelator, Everybody’s Talkin’, Made Up Mind, and Let Me Get By.
